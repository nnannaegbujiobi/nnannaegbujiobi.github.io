Introduction

Spending a decade developing programs and methods to solve fitness problems led me to become a software engineer. I thrive in identifying a problem, working like hell to create a solution; for me, web development brings on a natural high. Whether it’s putting together bootcamps to get brides ready for their big day or a pesky algorithm on a project I'll be there in the trenches creating the solutions to problems. The human body is as complex as any programming language. Discovering the logic or algorithm to unlock an individual's ability to change their bodies is how we succeed in the fitness industry. I have spent a decade writing these algorithms. Let's create a conditional: if a = client, subtracts carbs from their diet and increases workouts/higher protein intake the return would be weight loss. It may seem pretty simple but in-fact it’s much more complicated. Like any programming language or software there are similarities but differences that make getting the desired outcome frustrating. For example cardio and weight-lifting are exercises but they do not yield the same output. They differ in how they process nutrition as well as the effect on your body and muscles.
